## What Is This System?

The CEPA e-Permitting System is an online platform that replaces the traditional paper-based permit application process. Instead of physical forms, manual tracking, and in-person submissions, applicants can now apply for environmental permits through a website, and CEPA staff can process these applications digitally.

**In simple terms:** This is CEPA's digital front door for anyone who needs an environmental permit in Papua New Guinea.

---

## Who Uses This System?

### 1. The Public (Applicants)
Companies, individuals, and organizations who need environmental permits. They can:
- Register their business details online
- Submit permit applications from anywhere with internet
- Upload required documents digitally
- Track their application status in real-time
- Pay fees online
- Receive notifications about their applications

### 2. CEPA Staff
Different departments handle different stages of the application:

| Department | What They Do |
|------------|--------------|
| **Registry Unit** | Receives applications, checks if all documents are complete, assigns applications to officers |
| **Compliance Unit** | Reviews applications against environmental regulations, conducts site inspections |
| **Revenue Unit** | Calculates fees, generates invoices, tracks payments |
| **Directorate** | Makes final approval or rejection decisions, signs official letters |

---

## How Does the Process Work?

### Step 1: Registration
An applicant creates an account and registers their company or organization in the system.

### Step 2: Intent Registration
Before applying for a permit, applicants must first register their "intent" - basically telling CEPA what activity they plan to do and where. This helps CEPA understand the project scope early.

### Step 3: Permit Application
Once the intent is approved, applicants can submit a full permit application with:
- Project details and location (shown on a map)
- Environmental impact information
- Required supporting documents
- Activity-specific information (e.g., for mining, waste disposal, water extraction, etc.)

### Step 4: Internal Review
The application moves through CEPA departments:
1. **Registry** checks completeness
2. **Compliance** reviews environmental requirements
3. **Revenue** calculates and collects fees
4. **Directorate** makes the final decision

### Step 5: Decision
The application is either approved (permit issued) or rejected with reasons.
